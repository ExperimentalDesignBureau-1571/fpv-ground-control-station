# Universal Case and Station Body

The design of the universal case is developed as a balanced solution for transporting and deploying an FPV drone ground control station in field conditions. The primary focus of the design is on combining reliability, mobility, and manufacturing accessibility.

<img width="600" alt="General view of finished cases" src="picture/general_view_of_finished_cases.jpg" />

## Key Design Features:
<ul>
<li><b>Compactness and Mobility:</b> The body dimensions are optimized to ensure maximum operator mobility. The compact form factor allows the station to be conveniently transported in a backpack or as a separate small-sized equipment unit.</li>

<li><b>Optimization for Additive Manufacturing:</b> The geometry of the parts is designed for 3D printing on most common FDM printers. The maximum dimensions of individual elements do not exceed 250 × 250 × 250 mm, making the project accessible for implementation on budget equipment.</li>

<li><b>Economic Efficiency:</b> The development follows the principle of maximizing production cost reduction. The use of affordable materials (plastics like PETG/ABS) and standard hardware (fasteners) significantly lowers the cost of the finished product without sacrificing functionality.</li>

<li><b>Operational Durability and Repeatability:</b> The structure has a sufficient safety margin for intensive use. Technical solutions allow for easy replication of the body in local or field workshops.</li>

<li><b>Modular Architecture and Maintainability:</b></li>
<ul>
<li>The body consists of individual interchangeable modules.</li>

<li>High maintainability: in case of mechanical damage, it is sufficient to replace only the specific affected element.</li>

<li>Standardization of fasteners: Most connections are implemented using standard metric fasteners (M3 screws and nuts), simplifying assembly and component logistics.</li>
</ul>
</ul>
